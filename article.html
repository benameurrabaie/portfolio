Project 1

Federated Learning for IoT Intrusion Detection

Type: Research Project
Publication Date: June 2025

This project explores the integration of Federated Learning and Continual Learning for enhancing Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) in Internet of Things (IoT) environments. The goal is to improve detection accuracy and adaptability without compromising user privacy.

Our methodology involves the use of client-side updates and aggregation techniques that ensure differential privacy. Continual learning strategies such as Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC) and Learning without Forgetting (LwF) are embedded into the client training loop to mitigate catastrophic forgetting.

Evaluation was conducted on benchmark IoT datasets under varying attack scenarios. The results show significant improvements in both precision and recall, with up to 94.3% detection accuracy. The federated design also enables scalability to hundreds of distributed nodes.

In conclusion, the framework offers a privacy-preserving, adaptive, and efficient solution for modern intrusion detection in IoT networks. Future work will explore integration with homomorphic encryption and model compression techniques for low-resource devices.
