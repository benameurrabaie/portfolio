FCL-IWQMS: Federated Continual Learning for Adaptive Real-Time Water Quality Monitoring

Type: IoT, AI, Federated Learning, Continual Learning, Water Quality, Environmental AI
Publication Date: June 2025

The FCL-IWQMS project presents a robust framework for adaptive, real-time surface water quality monitoring. It combines IoT sensor technologies with Federated Continual Learning (FCL) to predict key water quality indicators such as dissolved oxygen, pH, turbidity, conductivity, and temperature while preserving data privacy and enabling decentralized intelligence.

The hardware platform is based on a compact, solar-powered IoT system integrating low-cost sensors (pH, DO, EC, turbidity, and temperature) with a Raspberry Pi LoRa gateway. The node transmits data hourly using LoRaWAN and operates autonomously for over two years in off-grid river environments.

On the software side, the system leverages continual learning techniques—PER (Prioritized Experience Replay), LWF (Learning Without Forgetting), and EWC (Elastic Weight Consolidation)—to adapt local models to new data while retaining past knowledge. These are combined with federated learning strategies—FedAvg, FedTM (Trimmed Mean), and AMU (Agnostic Model Update)—to aggregate updates across distributed clients securely.

The learning architecture combines 1D CNNs, BiLSTM layers, and an attention mechanism to extract spatial-temporal patterns from water quality streams. It uses MSE loss and the Adam optimizer with dropout and batch normalization to enhance generalization and reduce overfitting.

Experiments conducted on a public dataset from Nova Scotia’s Automated Water Quality Monitoring Network validate the system's performance across five river stations with 8 federated clients. The results show FedAvg-PER achieving best results for DO (MAE: 0.0536, R²: 0.908), while AMU-LWF excelled in pH prediction (MAE: 0.0397, R²: 0.7341). FedTM-LWF led water temperature and turbidity predictions, and FedTM-PER showed strong performance on conductivity.

As an integral member of the project, I contributed to the co-design and deployment of the IoT sensor kits, implemented the attention-enhanced CNN-BiLSTM architecture, and conducted experiments in Google Colab using PyTorch. I also analyzed model convergence trends and comparative performance across FL/CL configurations.

In summary, FCL-IWQMS offers a scalable, privacy-preserving, and efficient framework for intelligent water quality monitoring. Future work will include real-world validation of the platform in the Marne River (France), dynamic client participation, and the deployment of energy-aware adaptive learning mechanisms in natural ecosystems.
