Project 3

Autonomous Trading Agents in Cryptocurrency Markets Using Deep Reinforcement Learning

Type: Autonomous Trading Agents,Deep Reinforcement Learning, Cryptocurrency, AI
Publication Date: June 2025

This project explores the design and evaluation of autonomous trading agents in cryptocurrency markets using Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL). The study compares three prominent algorithms—Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO), Advantage Actor-Critic (A2C), and Double Deep Q-Network (DDQN)—within a simulated Bitcoin trading environment.

A Markov Decision Process (MDP) framework was employed to formalize the trading problem, integrating state observations from market indicators, trading actions (Buy, Sell, Hold), and reward signals based on portfolio performance. The system is trained on hourly BTC/USD historical data, preprocessed with engineered features like normalized volumes and price changes.

Each DRL agent adopts a distinct architecture: PPO and A2C utilize actor-critic networks with separate policy and value functions, while DDQN applies a dual-network Q-learning approach. PPO models feature clipped objective functions for stable updates; A2C leverages advantage estimates; and DDQN mitigates overestimation with target networks.

Experimental results indicate PPO provides the most stable and profitable performance, with a portfolio return of 1373.27%, followed by DDQN (815.38%) and A2C (722.63%). DDQN achieves the fastest convergence in just 35 episodes, while A2C suffers from high variance and lower reward consistency. PPO also achieves the highest average reward and success rate under tested conditions.

The evaluation includes key performance metrics: average reward, maximum reward, reward standard deviation, steps to convergence, and success rate. PPO shows robustness across different learning rates, while A2C requires fine-tuning to overcome instability. DDQN performs well but is sensitive to hyperparameters.

As a core contributor, I led the design of the PPO-based agent, implemented environment interaction policies, and conducted extensive experiments using PyTorch on Google Colab. I analyzed training curves, portfolio performance, and model robustness, contributing significantly to comparative benchmarking and result interpretation.

In summary, this project demonstrates that DRL offers a powerful approach to developing intelligent, adaptive, and profitable trading agents for volatile markets like cryptocurrency. Future extensions will include federated learning for decentralized agent collaboration, and enhanced modeling of risk and sentiment to further refine trading decisions.
